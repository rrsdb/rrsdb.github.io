$$ F(q) := \sum_{n=0}^\infty r(n)q^n :=\frac{f\left(-q^5,-q^7\right)}{f\left(-q^4\right)} = \frac{\left(q^5;q^{12}\right){}_{\infty } \left(q^7;q^{12}\right){}_{\infty }}{\left(q^4;q^{12}\right){}_{\infty } \left(q^8;q^{12}\right){}_{\infty }}$$

## Combinatorial interpretation of product

- $(-1)^n r(n)$ is the number of [overpartitions](../partitions.html#overpartitions) of $n$ into where overlined parts are $\equiv\pm 5\pmod{12}$ nonoverlined parts are $\equiv \pm 4\pmod{12}$.

## [Asymptotic formula](../asymptotics.html)

$ \displaystyle (-1)^n r(n) \sim\frac{e^{\frac{\pi  \sqrt{n}}{\sqrt{6}}}}{2\ 6^{3/4} n^{3/4}}$ as $n\to\infty. $
