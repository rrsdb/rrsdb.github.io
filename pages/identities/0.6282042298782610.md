# Article for infinite product with [signature](../product_signature.html) 10:(-1,1,-1,1,0,1,-1,1,-1,0)

!!graphs

$$ F(q) := \sum_{n=0}^\infty r(n)q^n :=\frac{f\left(1,q^5\right)}{2 \psi (q)} = \frac{(q^{},q^{3},q^{7},q^{9}; q^{10})_\infty}{(q^{2},q^{4},q^{6},q^{8}; q^{10})_\infty}$$

## Combinatorial interpretation of product

- $(-1)^n r(n)$ is the number of [partitions](../partitions.html#integer_partitions) of $n$ into parts where odd parts are $\pm 1, \pm 3\pmod{10}$, even parts are $\pm 2, \pm 4\pmod{10}$ and no odd part is repeated.

## [Asymptotic formula](../asymptotics.html)

$ \displaystyle (-1)^n r(n) \sim\frac{e^{\sqrt{\frac{2}{5}} \pi  \sqrt{n}}}{2 \sqrt[4]{2} 5^{3/4} n^{3/4}}$ as $n\to\infty. $
