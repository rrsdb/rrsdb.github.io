
# Article for infinite product with [signature](../product_signature.html) 9:(1,1,1,1,1,1,1,1,0)

!!graphs

$$ F(q) := \sum_{n=0}^\infty r(n)q^n :=\frac{f\left(-q^9,-q^{18}\right)}{f(-q)} = \frac{1}{(q^{},q^{2},q^{3},q^{4},q^{5},q^{6},q^{7},q^{8}; q^{9})_\infty}
$$

## Combinatorial interpretation of product

- $r(n)$ is the number of [partitions](../partitions.html#integer_partitions) of $n$ into non-multiples of $9$.

## [Asymptotic formula](../asymptotics.html)

$ \displaystyle r(n) \sim\frac{e^{\frac{4 \pi  \sqrt{n}}{3 \sqrt{3}}} \csc \left(\frac{\pi }{9}\right) \csc \left(\frac{2 \pi }{9}\right) \sec \left(\frac{\pi }{18}\right)}{24 \sqrt{2} \sqrt[4]{3} n^{3/4}}$ as $n\to\infty. $
