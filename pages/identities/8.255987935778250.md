# Article for infinite product with [signature](../product_signature.html) 2:(2,1)

!!graphs

$$ F(q) := \sum_{n=0}^\infty r(n)q^n :=\frac{1}{\varphi (-q)} = \frac{(-q;q)_\infty}{(q;q)_\infty} = \frac{1}{\left(q^2;q^2\right){}_{\infty } \left(q;q^2\right)_{\infty }^2}$$

## Combinatorial interpretation of product

- $r(n)$ is the number of [overpartitions](../partitions.html#overpartitions) of $n$.

## [Asymptotic formula](../asymptotics.html)

$ \displaystyle r(n) \sim\frac{e^{\pi  \sqrt{n}}}{8 n}$ as $n\to\infty. $  Due to H. S. Zuckerman [Z39] (1939, p. 31, Eq. (8.53))

## $q$-series representations

$ \displaystyle F(q) = \sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{ q^{n(n+1)/2} (-1;q)_n  }{ (q;q)_n^2 } $

Insert Bailey pair H(6) into the [1st op-Bailey lemma](../Bailey_pairs.html#op1_Bailey_lemma), and apply the [triple product identity](../q-series.html#triple_product).
