!!graphs

$$ F(q) := \sum_{n=0}^\infty r(n)q^n := \frac{H(-q)}{\chi(-q)} = \frac{Q\left(q^{10},-q^3\right)}{f(-q)} = \frac{1}{(q^{},q^{2},q^{5},q^{6},q^{8},q^{9},q^{11},q^{12},q^{14},q^{15},q^{18},q^{19}; q^{20})_\infty}$$

## Combinatorial interpretation of product

- $r(n)$ is the number of [partitions](../partitions.html#integer_partitions) of $n$ into parts congruent to $\pm 1, \pm 2, \pm 5, \pm 6, \pm 9 \pmod{20}$.

## [Asymptotic formula](../asymptotics.html)

$ \displaystyle r(n) \sim\frac{e^{\sqrt{\frac{2}{5}} \pi  \sqrt{n}}}{\sqrt[4]{10} \left(\sqrt{5}-1\right) \sqrt{5+\sqrt{5}} n^{3/4}}$ as $n\to\infty. $

## $q$-series representations

$ \displaystyle F(q) = \sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{ q^{n(n+1)}  }{ (q;q)_{2n+1} } $

**[Bailey pair](../Bailey_pairs.html) proof:**
 > Insert Bailey Pair A(2) into the [p-Bailey lemma](../Bailey_pairs.html#p_Bailey_lemma), and apply the [triple product identity](../q-series.html#triple_product) followed by the [quintuple product identity](../q-series.html#quintuple_product).

**Combinatorial interpretations:**
- $r(n)$ is the number of partitions $(a_1, a_2, a_3, \dots)$ of $n$ where $a_1 \geq 2 > a_2 \geq a_3 > a_4 \geq \cdots$.  Due to Hirschhorn [H79] (p. 33, Theorem 2).
