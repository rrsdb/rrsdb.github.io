Article for infinite product with signature 2:(1,0)

\[ F(q) := \sum_{n=0}^\infty r(n)q^n := \frac{f(-q)}{\varphi(-q)} = \frac{1}{(q;q^2)_\infty} = (-q;q)_\infty\]

Combinatorial interpretation of product

\(r(n)\) is the number of partitions of \(n\) into odd parts.

\(r(n)\) is the number of partitions of \(n\) with parts distinct.

Asymptotic formula for \(r(n)\)

\(\displaystyle r(n) \sim \frac{e^{\frac{\pi \sqrt{n}}{\sqrt{3}}}}{4 \sqrt[4]{3} n^{3/4}} \) as \( n\to\infty. \)
