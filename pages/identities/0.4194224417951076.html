Article for infinite product with signature 2:(-1,0)

\[ F(q) := \sum_{n=0}^\infty r(n)q^n := \frac{f(-q,-q)}{f(-q)} = (q;q^2)_\infty\]

Combinatorial interpretation of product

Let \(e(n)\) and \(o(n)\) count respectively the number of partitions of \(n\) with an even and odd number of distinct, odd parts. Then \(r(n) = e(n) - o(n)\).

\(q\)-series representations

\(\displaystyle F(q) = \sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{(-1)^nq^{n^2}}{(q^2;q^2)_n}\)

Bailey pair proof: PROOF HERE OR DELETE THIS LINE

Combinatorial interpretation: The term \((-1)^nq^{n^2}/(q^2;q^2)_n\) for each index \(n \geq 0\) generates partitions with exactly \(n\) distinct, odd parts, with a positive or negative sign if \(n\) is even or odd, respectively. This can be seen from the fact that \(1 + 3 + 5 + \cdots + 2n-1 = n^2\) for \(n \geq 1\).
