$$ F(q) := \sum_{n=0}^\infty r(n)q^n :=\frac{f\left(-q^{12},-q^{15}\right)}{(q;q)_{\infty }} = \frac{1}{(q^{},q^{2},q^{3},q^{4},q^{5},q^{6},q^{7},q^{8},q^{9},q^{10},q^{11},q^{13},q^{14},q^{16},q^{17},q^{18},q^{19},q^{20},q^{21},q^{22},q^{23},q^{24},q^{25},q^{26}; q^{27})_\infty}$$

## Combinatorial interpretation of product

- $r(n)$ is the number of [partitions](../partitions.html#integer_partitions) of $n$ into parts not congruent to $0, \pm 12\pmod{27}$.

## [Asymptotic formula](../asymptotics.html)

$ \displaystyle r(n) \sim\frac{e^{\frac{4 \pi  \sqrt{n}}{3 \sqrt{3}}} \csc \left(\frac{\pi }{27}\right) \csc \left(\frac{2 \pi }{27}\right) \csc \left(\frac{\pi }{9}\right) \csc \left(\frac{4 \pi }{27}\right) \csc \left(\frac{5 \pi }{27}\right) \csc \left(\frac{2 \pi }{9}\right) \sec \left(\frac{\pi }{54}\right) \sec \left(\frac{5 \pi }{54}\right) \sec \left(\frac{7 \pi }{54}\right) \sec \left(\frac{11 \pi }{54}\right) \sec \left(\frac{13 \pi }{54}\right)}{6144 \sqrt{2} \sqrt[4]{3} n^{3/4}}$ as $n\to\infty. $
