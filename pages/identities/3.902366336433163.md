# Article for infinite product with [signature](../product_signature.html) SIGNATURE HERE

!!graphs

$$ F(q) := \sum_{n=0}^\infty r(n)q^n := \frac{ f(-q,-q^5) }{  \varphi(-q) } = \frac{1}{(q^{},q^{2},q^{4},q^{5}; q^{6})_\infty(q^{3}; q^{6})_\infty^{2}} $$

## Combinatorial interpretation of product

- $r(n)$ is the number of [two-colored partitions](../partitions.html#colored_partitions) of $n$ where blue parts are not multiples of 6 and red parts are congruent to $3\pmod{6}$.
- $r(n)$ is the number of [overpartitions](../partitions.html#overpartitions) of $n$ where nonoverlined parts are congruent to $\pm 2, 3 \pmod{6}$.

## [Asymptotic formula](../asymptotics.html)

$ \displaystyle r(n) \sim \frac{e^{\sqrt{\frac{2}{3}} \pi  \sqrt{n}}}{4 \sqrt[4]{2} 3^{3/4} n^{3/4}} $ as $ n\to\infty. $

## $q$-series representations

$ \displaystyle F(q) = \sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{ q^{n(n+1)} (-q;q)_n }{ (q;q^2)_{n+1} (q;q)_n } $
