
# Article for infinite product with [signature](../product_signature.html) 6:(2,1,2,1,2,0)


$$ F(q) := \sum_{n=0}^\infty r(n)q^n :=\frac{f\left(-q^6,-q^{12}\right)}{\varphi (-q)} = \frac{(q^6;q^6)_\infty (-q;q)_\infty}{(q;q)_\infty} = \frac{1}{\left(q^2;q^6\right){}_{\infty } \left(q^4;q^6\right){}_{\infty } \left(q;q^6\right)_{\infty }^2 \left(q^3;q^6\right)_{\infty }^2 \left(q^5;q^6\right)_{\infty }^2}$$

## Combinatorial interpretation of product

- $r(n)$ is the number of [overpartitions](../partitions.html#overpartitions) of $n$ into where nonoverlined parts are not multiples of $6$.

## [Asymptotic formula](../asymptotics.html)

$ \displaystyle r(n) \sim\frac{e^{\frac{2}{3} \sqrt{2} \pi  \sqrt{n}}}{6\ 2^{3/4} n^{3/4}}$ as $n\to\infty. $
