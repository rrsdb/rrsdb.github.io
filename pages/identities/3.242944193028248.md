
# Article for infinite product with [signature](../product_signature.html) 14:(1,1,1,0,1,1,1,1,1,0,1,1,1,0)

!!graphs

$$ F(q) := \sum_{n=0}^\infty r(n)q^n :=\frac{f\left(-q^4,-q^{10}\right)}{f(-q)} = \frac{1}{(q^{},q^{2},q^{3},q^{5},q^{6},q^{7},q^{8},q^{9},q^{11},q^{12},q^{13}; q^{14})_\infty}$$

## Combinatorial interpretation of product

- $r(n)$ is the number of [partitions](../partitions.html#integer_partitions) of $n$ into parts not congruent to $0, \pm 4\pmod{14}$.

## [Asymptotic formula](../asymptotics.html)

$ \displaystyle r(n) \sim\frac{\sqrt[4]{\frac{11}{21}} e^{\sqrt{\frac{11}{21}} \pi  \sqrt{n}} \sin \left(\frac{\pi }{14}\right) \csc ^2\left(\frac{\pi }{7}\right)}{8 n^{3/4}}$ as $n\to\infty. $
