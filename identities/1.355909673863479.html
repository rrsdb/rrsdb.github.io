Rogers–Ramanujan type identities

Article for infinite product with signature 4:(0,1,0,0)

\[ F(q) := \sum_{n=0}^\infty r(n)q^n := \frac{f(-q,-q^3)}{f(-q)} = \frac{1}{(q^2;q^4)_\infty} = (-q^2;q^2)_\infty \]

Combinatorial interpretation of product

  • \(r(n)\) is the number of partitions of \(n\) with only parts congruent to 2 modulo 4.

  • \(r(n)\) is the number of partitions of \(n\) with distinct parts, all even.

Asymptotic formula for \(r(n)\)

\(\displaystyle r(2n) \sim \frac{e^{\frac{\pi \sqrt{n}}{\sqrt{3}}}}{4\sqrt[4]{3} n^{3/4}}\) as \( n\to\infty. \) \( \displaystyle r(2n + 1) = 0 .\)

\(q\)-series representations

\(\displaystyle F(q) = \sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{q^{n(n+1)}}{(q^2;q^2)_n}\)

Bailey pair proof: Insert E(3) into the p-Bailey lemma and apply the triple product identity.

Combinatorial interpretations: The expression \(q^{n(n+1)/2}/(q;q)_n\) generates partitions with exactly \(n\) distinct parts. The terms of this sum are the same, dilated by a factor of 2.
